\section{Executive Summary}

Jan-Sunwai is the first module of Pollity.in, an AI-empowered Digital Chief of Staff for India's 3.3 million elected representatives. This PRD defines the requirements for \textbf{MVP v0} --- a working Phase 1 prototype to be demonstrated live at the SNVC Week 9 pitch in Spring 2026.

The prototype covers one use case end-to-end: a citizen sends a WhatsApp message, the system classifies it using AI, stores it, sends an acknowledgement, and displays it on a dashboard where office staff can track and update its status through closure.

\begin{notebox}
\textbf{This is a prototype, not a production system.} MVP v0 is designed to validate the core value proposition with a single demo office. Production hardening, multi-tenancy, and full Auth happen in Phase 2 (post-SNVC).
\end{notebox}

\section{Problem Statement}

\subsection{The Governance Gap}

India's elected representatives --- MPs, MLAs, municipal councillors, panchayat sarpanchs --- receive hundreds of constituent grievances per week through a chaotic mix of channels: WhatsApp messages, in-person visits, phone calls, letters, and social media. There is no standard system for managing these.

The result is predictable:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item Grievances are forgotten or lost between intake and resolution
  \item No visibility into how many grievances exist, their status, or which departments are responsible
  \item Citizens receive no acknowledgement or follow-up, eroding trust
  \item Representatives cannot demonstrate accountability --- they have no data on what they resolved
  \item Staff spend hours manually forwarding messages and chasing departments
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Specific Problem Jan-Sunwai Solves}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Pain Point} & \textbf{What Jan-Sunwai Does} \\
\midrule
Grievances arrive on personal WhatsApp, get buried & Dedicated office number, every message captured \\
No categorisation or prioritisation & Claude AI classifies by category and urgency instantly \\
No acknowledgement to citizen & Automatic Ref ID sent back within seconds \\
No tracking after intake & Dashboard with full lifecycle from registered to closed \\
No accountability data & Every grievance timestamped, assigned, and searchable \\
Duplicate grievances from same citizen & AI duplicate detection flags repeat submissions \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\subsection{Why Now}

WhatsApp Business API, capable AI classification at near-zero cost (Claude Haiku at \$0.00026 per grievance), and managed Postgres (Supabase free tier) make this buildable under \$500 total. This was not economically feasible two years ago.

\section{Users and Personas}

\begin{notebox}
Jan-Sunwai is \textbf{office-facing, not citizen-facing.} Citizens interact only through WhatsApp (intake and status updates). They never log in to the system.
\end{notebox}

\vspace{0.8em}

\subsection{Primary User: Office Staff}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=pollityblue, sharp corners]
\textbf{Who:} Personal Assistant (PA) or Constituency Manager in the representative's office \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Tech comfort:} Uses WhatsApp daily; comfortable with Google Sheets; has never used a CRM or SaaS dashboard \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Primary job:} Receives grievances, forwards them to departments, follows up, reports back to the representative \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Key frustration:} Loses track of grievances after forwarding; citizens call back asking for status; has no way to show the representative what was done \\[0.3em]
\textbf{What they need from Jan-Sunwai:} See all open grievances in one place; update status in 3 clicks; know what needs action today
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Secondary User: Elected Representative}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=pollityblue, sharp corners]
\textbf{Who:} MLA, MP, municipal councillor, or panchayat sarpanch \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Tech comfort:} Uses WhatsApp; may not use a laptop regularly \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Primary job:} Constituent service, legislative work, political engagement \\[0.3em]
\textbf{Key frustration:} Cannot answer ``how many grievances did we resolve this month?'' Cannot hold staff accountable without data \\[0.3em]
\textbf{What they need from Jan-Sunwai:} A weekly summary of grievance resolution; a number they can quote publicly; confidence that nothing is falling through the cracks
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Non-User: Citizen}

The citizen is a \textbf{touchpoint}, not a user. They interact via WhatsApp only:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Input:} Sends a grievance message in any language
  \item \textbf{Output:} Receives an acknowledgement with a Reference ID
  \item They do not log in, do not see the dashboard, and do not have an account
\end{itemize}

FR1.4 & \must   & System must process text messages; non-text messages (images, audio) are silently ignored in v0 & C1 \\
FR1.5 & \must   & System must identify which office a message belongs to using the WhatsApp phone number ID & S1 \\

\subsection{FR6 --- Grievance Lifecycle}

The following status transitions are supported. Staff move grievances forward manually through the dashboard.

\begin{center}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray, colframe=pollityblue, sharp corners]
\small\ttfamily
registered $\to$ acknowledged $\to$ assigned $\to$ in\_progress $\to$ resolved $\to$ verified $\to$ closed
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{center}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Status} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
registered   & Grievance received and classified. Ref ID issued to citizen. \\
acknowledged & Office has reviewed the grievance. \\
assigned     & Forwarded to a department or staff member. \\
in\_progress & Department has confirmed receipt and is working on it. \\
resolved     & Department reports the issue is fixed. \\
verified     & Office has independently confirmed resolution (not just paper). \\
closed       & Case closed. Citizen may be notified (Phase 2). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\section{Open Questions}

The following questions are unresolved as of this version of the PRD. They must be answered before Phase 2 planning.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Pricing:} What is the right monthly price for a beta user? Options under consideration: \$0 (free beta) / \$10 / \$25 per office per month.

  \item \textbf{Language:} Should the acknowledgement message to citizens be sent in the same language as their message? Requires Claude to translate the ACK --- adds latency and cost.

  \item \textbf{Phone number:} Will each office get their own WhatsApp number, or share one? Separate numbers are cleaner for citizens but require more Meta WABA management.

  \item \textbf{Onboarding:} How do we onboard 50 beta offices post-SNVC? Manual white-glove, or self-serve?

  \item \textbf{Indian data residency:} DPDP Act requires data localisation. Railway currently runs on US/EU servers. Do we need to host on AWS Mumbai before Phase 2 beta, or after seed?

  \item \textbf{WhatsApp template messages:} For status updates to citizens (Phase 2), Meta requires pre-approved message templates. When do we apply for approval --- during Phase 1 or at start of Phase 2?
\end{enumerate}
